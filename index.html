About Me

I am currently a M.Sc student in Statistical Machine Learning at the University of Alberta. I am working under the supervision of Dr. Alona Fyshe, Dr. Adam Kashlak, and Dr. Martha White, and am affiliated with Amii. I have a B.Sc in mathematics as well as a B.SocSc in political science and economics. My research interests lie broadly within machine learning and, more specifically, in understanding the mathematical principles behind neural networks.

Other Interests

I have a very diverse array of interests and have largely failed to narrow my interests over the years. I am passionate about justice, whether it be political, social, economic, or legal, and am fascinated by philosophy, political and legal theory in particular. I am also very pre-occupied with the transformative power of technology - the internet and artifical intelligence in particular - and the effects it has on our society.

I love to travel, and have, at various times, lived in New Zealand, Central America, India, and all over Canada. I also love to hike and go backcountry camping. If I'm not travelling or in nature, I love to play just about any sport you can think of and enjoy reading both non-fiction and fiction. I'm also hopelessly addicted to podcasts.

Why the Alice in Wonderland theme?

I've always really enjoyed Alice in Wonderland and appreciated its carefree, freewheeling creativity combined with its subtle sophistication lurking just under the surface. I feel like the book really captures the curiosity and creativity that we all have as children, but that so many of us lose as we grow up. My life has been driven by my curiosity and awe at the world we live in, and I believe that we are surrounded by rabbit holes and that if we only have the courage to explore, we will find that they lead to wonderlands of beauty and fascination.
